Learning Philosophy

A few personal ideas that shape how I learn, collaborate, and build technical understanding.

Constructivist Preference

I personally prefer a constructivist learning approach because it encourages active engagement, exploration, and critical thinking.

At the same time, I see real value in blending multiple learning theories instead of relying on just one.

Cognition and Higher Education

I believe a combination of constructivism and cognitivism works especially well in higher education.

For me, good learning is not only about doing, but also about organizing knowledge, building mental models, and understanding why systems work.

Growth Through Different Stages

Looking back, I feel my K-12 learning experience was shaped by both behaviorism and constructivism.

Behaviorism felt more visible in elementary school, while middle and high school brought in more independence, discussion, and active knowledge building.

Online and Blended Learning

I have experience with online or blended learning courses at Rutgers and similar academic settings.

I believe meaningful student learning can be supported online when structure, communication, and collaboration are thoughtfully designed.

Technology Literacy and Collaboration
